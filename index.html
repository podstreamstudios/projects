Stratalab™ · Food + Drink Registers · RPV Processes Layer

TableSet

Mitacs · In Development

Hospitality booking platform pairing chef and bartender as matched professionals — the Food + Drink register companion to Megaband. TableSet addresses the variety engineering problem at the RPV Processes layer: resources (chefs, bartenders, kitchens) are assumed present; the unsolved problem is governing the encounter between professional cult value and guest preference without destroying what makes the professional irreplaceable.

The exchange value app is the attenuation mechanism — specifying the pattern (culinary concept matched to property and guest profile) while delegating content to local professional decision. Pilot property: SeaBaby Estates, Summerlin NV — Blau + Associates as curatorial partner. Beer (1973) Designing Freedom as primary theoretical source — Canadian provenance via CBC Massey Lectures.

VICSS — VanIsle Craft Spirits Society

RTRI · Mitacs Pipeline

The VanIsle Craft Spirits Society is the dissertation's Chapter 5 applied ecosystem — all three RPV lenses operating simultaneously in uncontrolled conditions. VICSS coordinates Vancouver Island craft distillers through a cooperative governance framework, converting competitive variety among distillers into structural collaboration.

Two-level Beer/Ashby attenuation: Level 1 — Regulatory attenuation (LCRB, DFO water rights, zoning, heritage, capital requirements) reduces the on-site distillery concept to white-label production at an established Vancouver Island distillery. Level 2 — VICSS cooperative governance attenuates competitive variety among Island distillers into structural collaboration. The Clearwater Spirits five-expression brand family instantiates the |F|D|S|L| schema at the product design level — each expression a Wartofsky tertiary artifact encoding the collective memory of a site.

MML Partnership · VICSS · VanIsle · Campbell River · Light Register

Lodge and Lab — AI Previsualization for Heritage Hospitality

Mitacs · Pipeline

Mitacs Accelerate Entrepreneur application with Multimodal Media Lab (MML, Vancouver) as industry partner. Research question: what systematic AI previsualization methodology creates measurable decision-making and marketing value for small heritage hospitality proprietors — and under what conditions does that methodology generate cross-promotional reach that extends beyond the immediate client relationship?

Case study: Campbell River Lodge / Goose & Gander, Campbell River BC — previsualized as Lodge and Lab, with River Lodge Whisky and the Clearwater Spirits distillery concept as the brand content. The previsualization suite functions simultaneously as a feasibility instrument (evaluating on-site distillery viability before capital commitment), a marketing asset (the renders are the tourism promotion), and an MML portfolio case study (generating discovery reach for the Campbell River region among MML's hospitality client network). A four-venue ClearLED integration evaluation — Campbell River Lodge, Naturally Pacific Resort, Match Public House / Chances Casino, Painter's Lodge — forms the comparative research component.

Outputs: peer-reviewed paper submitted to TIM Review (Carleton); previsualization deliverable suite D1–D9 (bottle/label, interior renders, stage design, ClearLED integration, distillery vision); methodology documentation for replication across BC destination hospitality venues.
