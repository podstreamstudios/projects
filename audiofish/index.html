The format

A new social format for mixed audiences

Part pub quiz, part card game, part karaoke — or even live band karaoke, if you want to add your own local music community. Audiofish is the first format where trivia players and performers are in the same room, playing the same game from different angles.

Pub quiz excludes performers. Karaoke excludes the audience that wants to participate without singing. Music bingo is passive — you listen and mark a card. Audiofish connects all three into a single continuous experience where every person in the venue has a reason to be there.

Tactics

Music trivia rounds

All participants compete — no limit. Teams answer questions across 13 themed artist months. Every correct answer earns credits. Deeper cultural knowledge earns more: album titles, key lyrics, soundtrack connections, seasonal associations.

Strategy

The Audiofish card game

Credits buy access to the table. Six singers play Go Fish — card ranks map to themed artist months. Collecting four cards of the same rank earns a performance slot. Singers negotiate, coalition-build, compete for the right to sing.

Goals

Live performance — or cash out

Complete a book: choose your song and perform it. Or cash out credits for prizes. Or pledge them to a friend. Or donate to HeadsUpGuys. The credits economy governs how value flows through the room.

The format works with any existing karaoke host's song catalogue — Singa, KaraFun, a laptop library — and requires no live band to get started. Add a musician community later and you have something different again: the AI voice host coordinating a live band assembled song by song from registered local musicians. That's the advanced tier. The game mechanics are identical.

The 13 card ranks

The Rockaoke themed months

The card ranks were developed over a decade of live events by Rockaoke Live! feat. The Naturals — a Vancouver live band karaoke platform, 2013–2024 — and documented across 12 Spotify playlists. Each rank is a cultural field, not a strict artist discography: songs qualify by artist name, album title, key lyric, soundtrack association, or seasonal cultural calendar. Mapping 12 months onto 13 card ranks required one solution: May splits into two, and Queen cards are wild.

The ecosystem

Four structural partners

Fish.audio
AI voice infrastructure

Powers the AI voice host. The Audiofish brand name echoes the Fish.audio product at the platform's identity level. Multilingual capability enables global licensed deployment without English-language host dependency.

Singa
Karaoke catalogue

The song catalogue layer. The Audiofish card deck is generated from Singa's available songs organized by the 13 themed month ranks. Direct access to KJs and venue operators who make platform purchasing decisions. For songs not yet part of Singa's catalogue, Musical AI — which raised $4.5M in January 2025 to build attribution and licensing infrastructure for AI-assisted music platforms — provides the rights management layer that makes expanding the catalogue feasible at scale.

Soundtrap
Onboarding + community development

Authentication and onboarding layer. Audiofish registration automatically creates a Soundtrap account; existing users sign in with Soundtrap credentials. A creative audio workspace for building original content and nurturing the local music communities — singers and musicians — that make future versions of Audiofish possible... but with a live band on demand!

HeadsUpGuys
Confirmed charity partner

The world's leading men's mental health resource — 5M+ global visitors, 10,000 weekly users in 50+ countries, funded entirely by private donations. The donation mechanic is built into the credits economy: participants pledge credits to HeadsUpGuys with a single tap. The venue music audience is predominantly male. The format's community-building design is the kind of supportive social environment HeadsUpGuys advocates for.

DNDA
Methodology partner

Don Norman Design Award — a US 501(c)(3) public charity and global Humanity-Centered Design movement. Provides the HCD+ evaluative framework for the paper prototype and iterative design phases. Partners include the Nielsen Norman Group and UC San Diego Design Lab.

The research backing

Mitacs Accelerate Entrepreneur

Audiofish is the applied component of a research project submitted to Mitacs Accelerate Entrepreneur, in partnership with 1730 Technology Consulting (industry partner) and Carleton University's Sprott School of Business, TIM program (academic partner).

The research applies a theoretical correction to IDEO's widely adopted design thinking framework — substituting Christensen's Resources/Processes/Values (RPV) model — to the design of community platform ecosystems for live music events. The practitioner origin is a decade of operational experience with Rockaoke Live! feat. The Naturals in Vancouver (2013–2024): the platform that generated the 13 themed months, the catalogue methodology, and the diagnosis of why a single-band live karaoke format with 500+ songs eventually fails at the infrastructure layer.

"Find an activity setting where you can be both participant and analyst. Enter into the process of helping things grow in the activity system you have entered. Take your ability to create and sustain effective systems as evidence of your theory's adequacy." — Michael Cole, Cultural Psychology (1996), pp. 349–350

The BC proof-of-concept runs across two venue partners: The Alcove Lounge, East Vancouver (target: May–September) and Match Public House at Chances Casino, Campbell River (October–February). Paper prototype testing precedes digital deployment — the core game mechanics are validated with printed card decks and a whiteboard credits economy before any technology integration is required.

Sponsors are acknowledged in the peer-reviewed research output as infrastructure partners — academic visibility that outlasts any single event activation.
